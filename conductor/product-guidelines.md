# TunaOS - Product Guidelines

## Brand & Tone
TunaOS maintains a **Community-Integrated Identity**, acknowledging its roots in the [Bluefin](https://projectbluefin.io/) and [Universal Blue](https://universal-blue.org/) communities while carving out its own space on the AlmaLinux foundation.

The prose style is a blend of:
- **Technical & Professional:** Precise information for enterprise and power users.
- **Friendly & Accessible:** Using approachable language and clear icons to make complex tech more relatable.
- **Concise & Action-Oriented:** Getting straight to the point with actionable instructions.

## Documentation Guidelines
The primary focus of TunaOS documentation is on **User-Facing Guides**. We aim to make the onboarding process as seamless as possible for both enterprise professionals and general Linux enthusiasts.

- **Structure:** Clear headings, descriptive step-by-step instructions, and prominent use of warnings/notes.
- **Content:** Prioritize "how-to" guides and "getting started" information over purely theoretical deep dives.

## User Experience (UX) Philosophy
TunaOS follows a "Pragmatic Polish" approach to the desktop experience:
- **Balance:** We aim for a balance of features that most users will find genuinely useful while staying close to the stock experience where possible.
- **Value-Driven Deviations:** We are willing to depart from the "stock" feel if it adds significant value for the majority of users (e.g., pre-integrated Homebrew, Flathub, and specific GNOME extensions).
- **Predictability:** Maintaining the stability and reliability expected of an Enterprise Linux desktop remains the top priority.
